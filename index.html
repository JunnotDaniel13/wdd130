Antananarivo, Madagascar

Antananarivo, often referred to as "Tana," is the capital and largest city of Madagascar, located in the central highlands of the island. It serves as the political, economic, and cultural hub of the country, blending modern urban life with rich historical heritage. The city is known for its hilly terrain, colorful markets, and iconic Rova of Antananarivo, a royal palace complex that reflects its historical significance as the seat of the Merina monarchy. Despite its bustling atmosphere, Antananarivo faces challenges such as rapid urbanization and infrastructure development, but it remains a vibrant center of Malagasy culture and tradition.
